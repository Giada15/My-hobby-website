Some of the regular trails I explore around the Waterloo area include Bechtel Park, Kiwanis Park, the Waterloo Geo Time Trail, Columbia Lake, North Shore Park in Stratford, Woolwich Trails in St. Jacobs, Elora Gorge Trail in Elora, and Crown Land Trail. Each offers unique scenery and landscapes. The Stratford trails provide beautiful river views, perfect for a peaceful and reflective walk. I enjoy Bechtel Park because, even though it is located in the city, it is not frequently visited and offers wooded paths that are enjoyable in both winter and summer. At Crown Land Trail, the transition from the wooded path to a trail along agricultural fields provides a refreshing change of scenery. On the St. Jacobs trail, small birds come to be fed by people walking along the path.

The most memorable trails I’ve visited are Rockwood Conservation Area, Orangeville Conservation Area, and the Guelph Arboretum. I particularly enjoy Rockwood because it features diverse landscapes along the trail. I sometimes go canoeing on the lake, as the scenery is breathtaking, with rocky elements and caves shaping the panorama. The canoeing path ends at a beautiful waterfall.

Some of my most enjoyable moments have been spent in nature and on trails. It’s a refreshing, free therapy that has left me with many wonderful memories. Discovering new natural places brings me both peace and joy.
